How This Mortgage Calculator Works

This calculator uses the standard mortgage amortization formula for the principal & interest payment, then adds your escrow and optional costs for a true monthly total:

∑ Formula Mortgage Amortization (P&I)
M = P ×
r (1 + r)n
(1 + r)n − 1
M Monthly P&I payment
P Principal — home price minus down payment
r Monthly rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
n Total payments (Years × 12)

What's Included in Your Total Monthly Payment

  • Principal & Interest (P&I) — The base mortgage payment calculated by the amortization formula above.
  • Property Tax — Enter your annual tax bill; the calculator divides it by 12 for the monthly escrow portion.
  • Homeowner's Insurance — Annual premium divided by 12, added to your escrow payment.
  • PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) — Required when your down payment is less than 20%. Automatically removed once your equity reaches 20% of the home price.
  • HOA Fees — Monthly homeowners association fee, if applicable.
  • Extra Monthly Payment — Optional additional principal payment. The calculator shows exactly how much interest you save and how many months earlier you'll pay off the mortgage.

Input Field Guide

  • Home Price — The full purchase price of the property.
  • Down Payment — Enter as a dollar amount or percentage. Less than 20% triggers PMI. The calculator syncs both fields automatically.
  • Interest Rate (%) — Your annual mortgage rate (APR). Current 30-year fixed rates typically range from 6–8%.
  • Mortgage Term — Most common: 30 years (lower payment) or 15 years (less interest). The calculator supports any term from 1–50 years.
  • PMI Rate (%/yr) — Typically 0.2%–2% of the original mortgage amount annually. Your lender sets this. Leave at 0 if down payment ≥ 20%.
  • HOA/month — Check your community's HOA documents. Condos and planned communities often have monthly fees of $100–$500+.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is PITI in a mortgage payment?
PITI stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. It represents the full monthly mortgage obligation most lenders use to qualify borrowers. Our calculator shows each component separately and adds PMI and HOA if applicable, giving you the true total monthly housing cost.
When does PMI go away?
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is automatically removed when your mortgage balance drops to 80% of the home's original purchase price — i.e., when your equity reaches 20%. This calculator tracks your balance month by month and shows you exactly which month PMI is eliminated in the Equity Growth tab.
How much does a $400,000 mortgage cost per month?
For a $400,000 mortgage at 6.75% for 30 years, the P&I payment is approximately $2,594/month. Add typical escrow (taxes + insurance ≈ $500/mo) and you're looking at roughly $3,100/month before any PMI or HOA. Use the calculator above for your exact numbers.
Is it worth paying extra on your mortgage?
Yes — extra payments go 100% toward principal, which reduces the balance on which future interest is charged. On a 30-year $400,000 mortgage at 6.75%, paying just $200/month extra saves over $60,000 in interest and cuts nearly 5 years off the term. Enter your scenario in the calculator to see your exact savings.
How much should my down payment be?
20% is the traditional target because it eliminates PMI, but many mortgages allow 3%–5% down. A larger down payment means: lower monthly payment, less total interest, no PMI, and better mortgage terms. Use the Down Payment field to compare scenarios side by side.
When does it make sense to refinance?
Refinancing generally makes sense if you can lower your rate by at least 1%, plan to stay in the home longer than the break-even point (typically 18–24 months), and have enough equity. The Refi Alerts tab in the calculator estimates your monthly savings at a 1% rate reduction.
What's the difference between a 15-year and 30-year mortgage?
A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but significantly less total interest paid. A 30-year mortgage has lower payments but costs much more in total. For example: a $350,000 mortgage at 6.75% costs $2,441/mo over 30 years vs. $3,102/mo over 15 years — but the 30-year term costs approximately $200,000 more in total interest. Try both terms in the calculator to compare.
