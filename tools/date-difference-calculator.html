How Date Difference Is Calculated

Days = End Date − Start Date (in milliseconds ÷ 86,400,000)

Weeks = Days ÷ 7 (whole weeks + remaining days)
Months ≈ Days ÷ 30.4375 (average days per month)
Years ≈ Days ÷ 365.25 (accounting for leap years)

Example: January 1, 2024 to December 31, 2024
= 366 days (2024 is a leap year)
= 52 weeks, 2 days
= ~12 months
= ~1 year

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate the number of days between two dates?
Subtract the start date from the end date. In practice, convert both dates to the number of milliseconds since the Unix epoch (January 1, 1970), subtract, then divide by 86,400,000 (milliseconds per day). Our calculator handles this automatically — just pick your dates.
How many days are between January 1 and December 31?
In a non-leap year: 364 days. In a leap year (like 2024): 365 days. January 1 to December 31 of the same year spans the entire year except for January 1 itself (since we count from the start date to the end date, not including both endpoints by convention).
Can I calculate my age in days?
Yes! Enter your date of birth as the Start Date and today's date as the End Date. The result shows your exact age in days, weeks, months, and years. The average person lives approximately 27,375 days (75 years).
How many business days are between two dates?
Business days exclude weekends (and optionally holidays). To calculate: take total calendar days, subtract weekends (roughly × 5/7), and subtract any holidays in the period. For example, 30 calendar days has approximately 21–22 business days. This calculator shows calendar days only.
Why does the month/year result show "approximately"?
Months have different lengths (28–31 days) and years vary due to leap years. To avoid ambiguity, we use 30.4375 as the average days-per-month and 365.25 for years, which gives the most accurate approximation across all date ranges. For exact month counts, count the months on a calendar directly.
