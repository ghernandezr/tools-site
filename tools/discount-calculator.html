Discount Formulas Explained

1 Savings Amount
Savings = Original Price ×
Discount %

Example $149.99 × 30% = $149.99 × 0.30 = $45.00 saved
2 Final Price
Final Price = Original Price − Savings
Example $149.99 − $45.00 = $104.99 final price

Stacked Discounts

If a store applies two discounts in sequence (e.g., 20% off, then an additional 10% off), they do not add up to 30%. Calculate them sequentially:

! Stacked Discount Example
$100 → 20% off → $80 → 10% off → $72
Note Total effective discount is 28%, not 30%. Use the calculator twice in sequence.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a discount percentage?
Formula: Discount % = ((Original Price − Sale Price) ÷ Original Price) × 100. Example: item was $80, now $60 → ((80−60) ÷ 80) × 100 = 25% discount. Use our Percentage Calculator for this reverse calculation.
What is 20% off $75?
20% of $75 = $15 savings. Final price = $75 − $15 = $60. Quick mental trick: 20% off means you pay 80% of the original price. $75 × 0.80 = $60.
How do I find the original price from a discounted price?
Formula: Original Price = Sale Price ÷ (1 − Discount%). Example: You paid $70 after a 30% discount → Original = $70 ÷ 0.70 = $100. This is also called a "reverse discount" or "back-calculation."
Does the discount apply before or after tax?
In most US retail situations, discounts are applied to the pre-tax price. Sales tax is then calculated on the discounted price. Example: $100 item, 20% off = $80, then 8% tax = $86.40 final total. This calculator shows the pre-tax discounted price only.
Do stacked discounts add up?
No. Two sequential discounts of 20% and 10% give a combined discount of only 28%, not 30%. To calculate: apply the first discount to get an interim price, then apply the second discount to that interim price. The calculator above can be used twice in sequence for this.
