Discount Formula

Savings = Original Price × (Discount % ÷ 100)
Final Price = Original Price − Savings

Example: $149.99 at 30% off:
Savings = $149.99 × 0.30 = $45.00
Final Price = $149.99 − $45.00 = $104.99

Stacked Discounts

If a store applies two discounts in sequence (e.g., 20% off, then an additional 10% off), they do NOT add up to 30%. Calculate them sequentially:

$100 → 20% off → $80 → additional 10% off → $72
Total effective discount: 28% (not 30%)

Use the calculator twice in sequence for stacked discounts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate a discount percentage?
Formula: Discount % = ((Original Price − Sale Price) ÷ Original Price) × 100. Example: item was $80, now $60 → ((80−60) ÷ 80) × 100 = 25% discount. Use our Percentage Calculator for this calculation.
What is 20% off $75?
20% of $75 = $15 savings. Final price = $75 − $15 = $60. Quick mental trick: 20% off means you pay 80% of the original price. $75 × 0.80 = $60.
How do I find the original price from a discounted price?
Formula: Original Price = Sale Price ÷ (1 − Discount%). Example: You paid $70 after a 30% discount → Original = $70 ÷ 0.70 = $100. This is also called "reverse discount" calculation.
Does the discount apply before or after tax?
In most retail situations in the US, discounts are applied to the pre-tax price. Sales tax is then calculated on the discounted price. For example: $100 item, 20% off = $80, then 8% tax = $86.40 final total. This calculator shows the pre-tax discounted price only.
