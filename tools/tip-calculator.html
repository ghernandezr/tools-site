How Tip Is Calculated

Tip Amount = Bill × (Tip % ÷ 100)
Total Bill = Bill + Tip Amount
Per Person = Total Bill ÷ Number of People

Example: $85.50 bill, 20% tip, 3 people:
Tip = $85.50 × 0.20 = $17.10
Total = $85.50 + $17.10 = $102.60
Per person = $102.60 ÷ 3 = $34.20

Tip Percentage Guide

  • 10% — Below average. Use for genuinely poor service.
  • 15% — Standard. Acceptable for average service at sit-down restaurants.
  • 18% — Good. Common default for many restaurants that add gratuity automatically.
  • 20% — Great. The most common tip for good service in the US.
  • 25%+ — Exceptional service, or when you want to show extra appreciation.

Who Do You Tip?

  • Restaurant servers: 15–25% of pre-tax bill
  • Bartenders: $1–2 per drink, or 15–20%
  • Food delivery: 10–20%, minimum $3–5
  • Taxi / rideshare: 15–20%
  • Hotel housekeeping: $2–5/night
  • Hair salon: 15–20%

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I tip at a restaurant?
In the United States, tipping 18–20% is standard for good service at a sit-down restaurant. Tipping is calculated on the pre-tax subtotal in most cases, though some prefer to tip on the total including tax. For exceptional service, 25% or more is appropriate.
How do I calculate a 20% tip quickly in my head?
Move the decimal point one place left to find 10%, then double it for 20%. For a $75 bill: 10% = $7.50, so 20% = $15.00. For 15%, find 10% then add half of that: $7.50 + $3.75 = $11.25.
Should I tip on the pre-tax or post-tax amount?
There's no universal rule. Most people tip on the pre-tax subtotal, which is what the server actually provided service on. Some tip on the total bill for simplicity. The difference on a $50 bill with 8% tax and 20% tip is $0.80 — minimal either way.
What is a tip on a $100 bill at 20%?
20% of $100 = $20. Your total would be $120. Split among 4 people, each person pays $30 total ($25 for food + $5 tip per person).
