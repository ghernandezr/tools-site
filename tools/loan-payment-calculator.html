How This Loan Calculator Works

This loan payment calculator uses the standard loan amortization formula used by banks and financial institutions worldwide:

∑ Formula Loan Amortization Formula
M = P ×
r (1 + r)n
(1 + r)n − 1
M Monthly payment
P Principal — total amount borrowed
r Monthly interest rate (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
n Total payments (Years × 12)

Understanding Your Results

  • Monthly Payment — The fixed amount you pay each month for the full loan term.
  • Total Amount Paid — Monthly Payment × number of months. Includes principal + all interest.
  • Total Interest Paid — Total Amount Paid minus your original loan amount. This is the true cost of borrowing.

Input Field Guide

  • Loan Amount — The total amount you're borrowing. For auto loans, it's the car price minus any trade-in or down payment. For personal loans, it's the amount you receive.
  • Annual Interest Rate — Enter the annual percentage rate (APR) from your lender. Common rates: auto loans 4–8%, personal loans 6–36%.
  • Loan Term (Years) — How long you have to repay. Common terms: auto loans 3–7 years, personal loans 1–7 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my monthly loan payment?
Use the amortization formula: M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n−1], where P is the principal, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the number of monthly payments. Our calculator applies this formula automatically when you enter your inputs.
What is a good monthly payment for a $25,000 loan?
For a $25,000 loan at 6.5% APR over 5 years, the monthly payment is approximately $488.59. At 4% APR over the same term, it drops to about $460.41. Lower rates and longer terms reduce monthly payments but increase total interest paid.
What happens if I pay extra each month?
Extra principal payments reduce your outstanding balance faster, which means you pay less total interest and pay off the loan sooner. Enter an extra monthly amount in the Monthly Extra Payment field — the calculator will instantly show you how much interest you save and how many months earlier you'll pay off the loan.
What's the difference between APR and interest rate?
The interest rate is the cost of borrowing the principal only. APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the interest rate plus other loan costs (origination fees, broker fees, etc.). For this calculator, enter the APR if you want a more accurate total cost comparison, or the stated interest rate for the monthly payment calculation your lender provides.
How do I lower my monthly loan payment?
You can lower your monthly payment by:
  • Borrowing a smaller amount
  • Getting a lower interest rate (improve your credit score)
  • Extending your loan term (caution: this increases total interest paid)
  • Making a larger down payment on a secured loan
